What is Retail Automation?

Retail Automation is about using technology to improve customer satisfaction and experience during shopping in a supermarket as well as optimise tasks for the business such as product categorization and inventoryy management.
Self-service checkouts, where customres simply scan and pay for their items, remove the need for a checkout clerk to preform this tasks which can greatly optimise the speed of shoppers flowing in and out of the store, as a set amount of machines will decrease the wait times for a shopper to pay, and effectively removes lines, significantly increasing customer satisfaction and making it an effiecent use of both the shoppers and business time.

How is AI Being Implemented Into Retail?

AI's goal in retail is to automate mundane tasks, and to make the process of shopping in-store more efficient, to maximise profits for businesses and overall increase customers experience. AI is already being used in many simple ways, such as calculating the totals for the customers bills, including calculating prices of produce by weight which effectively removes any human error, or any risks of anyone being shortchanged, and can result in the business suffering a loss.

Notably, Amazon is pioneering a more extreme implication which they have coined "just-walk-out" technology which, in short, simply removes checkouts, removing lines, which makes it very convenient for the shopper.
The technology behind this revolves around Computer Vision connected to Machine Learning.
Computer Vision involves an intricate almost "spider web" of cameras located around the store, which has an entire vision of the store and customers entering and leaving.
The Computer has a virtual map of the store and can easily differentiate between different customers and map out where they are in the store. As the customer enters the store they scan a QR code, which sends a message to the server to create a virtual shopping cart for the customer.
The computer vision can then track the user's movements around the store, and when the shopper picks up an item, it can correctly identify what the item is and adds it to their virtual cart, based on where they are in the store. As the customer walks around the computer is constantly detecting if they are still carrying what they picked up, where they are. When the computer detects the shopper to have walked out, an invoice is sent to their Amazon account and Amazon is able to charge them from their credit card.
The customer is able to view their recepits through the Amazon app. This removes the need for checkouts, although some customers may wish to use a checkout, or speak to customer services, this is still available , and staff are still present to help any shopper that any needs of assistance.
