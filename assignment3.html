What is Retail Automation?

Retail Automation is about using technology to improve customer satisfaction and experience during shopping in a supermarket.
It eliminates lines, wait times, and reduces human errors, making it more efficient for both businesses and customers.

How is AI Being Implemented Into Retail?

AI's goal in retail is to automate mundane tasks, make the process of shopping in store more effiecient, to maximise profits for businesses and overall increase customers experience. Walmart,is irrelevant.

Notably, Amazon is pioneering a more extreme implication which they have coined "just-walk-out" technology which, in short, simply removes checkouts, removing lines, which makes it very convenient for the shopper.
The technology behind this revolves around Computer Vision connected to Machine Learning.
Computer Vision involves an intricate almost "spiderweb" of cameras located around the store, which has entire vision of the store and customers entering and leaving.
The Computer has a virtual map of the store and can easily differentiate between different customers and map out where they are in the store. As the customer enters the store they scan a qr code, which sends a message to the system to create a virtual shopping cart for the customer.
The computer vision can then track the users movements around the store, and when the shopper picks up an item, it can correctly identify what the item is and adds it to their virtual cart, based on where they are in the store. As the customer walks around the computer is constantly detecting if they are still carrying what they picked up, where they are, and when the customer, walks out, they simply are charged to their Amazon account where they can also see their receipt.
This removes the need for checkouts, although some customers may wish to use a checkout, or speak to customer services, this is still an option, and staff are still present to help any shopper that requires assistance.
